Playing Pig - Single Dice

Objective

The object of the simple dice game Pig is to be the first player to reach 100 points. Each player is given a turn to repeatedly roll the dice. However, after each successful roll (a roll other than 1), the player has to make a choice to either roll again or hold (not roll).

Rules

  • If the player rolls a 1, the player scores nothing that turn (ie loses the current tally for the turn) and the opponent player rolls.
  • If the player rolls a number other than 1, the number is added to the player's current tally (sum of the rolls during the turn) for the turn, and they have the option to continue to roll.
  • If the player holds, the player's turn tally is added to the player's overall score, and the opponent's turn begins.
  • The first player to 100 wins.

Strategy

The simple tactic to Pig is to view each roll as a bet that a 1 will not be rolled, knowing the odds of such a bet are 1 to 5. Then, the player has to ask how much should you risk versus how much is there to gain. According to Reiner Knizia in Dice Games Properly Explained, A successful throw produces one of the numbers 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6. On average, you will gain four points. If you put 20 points at stake this brings the odds to 4 to 20, that is 1 to 5, and makes a fair game. Whenever your accumulated points are less than 20, you should continue throwing, because the odds are in your favor.

However, he does list an exception to this strategy. Consider the following extreme example. Your opponent has a score of 99 and will likely win in the next turn. You have a score of 78 and a turn total of 20. Do you follow the "hold at 20" policy and end your turn with a score of 98? Why not? Because the probability of winning if you roll once more is higher than the probability of winning if the other player is allowed to roll.
